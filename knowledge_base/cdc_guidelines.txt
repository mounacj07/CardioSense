Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Recommendations for Heart Health:
- Regular monitoring of blood pressure is essential. Individuals with a reading over 130/80 mmHg are considered to have elevated blood pressure or hypertension stage 1.
- High blood cholesterol has no symptoms, so it must be checked with a blood test. A total cholesterol level of 240 mg/dL or higher is considered high and significantly increases heart disease risk.
- Maintaining a healthy weight, limiting alcohol intake, and managing stress are critical lifestyle modifications to reduce the risk of heart attacks and stroke.
- For individuals already identified as "at risk" of heart disease, medical professionals may prescribe statins to manage cholesterol or antihypertensives to manage blood pressure.
